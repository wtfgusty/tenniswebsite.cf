How was tennis created?

Location - France

Tennis, an adaptation of a very ancient sport, the jeu de paume, regulated in England in the 1870s, has become a popular sport with millions of followers every year. It was present at the Games from 1896 to 1924 before making its formal comeback in 1988, and the grand Olympic stage has become a pivotal milestone in the careers of the world's top tennis players.

How, and where can I play?

You can play at any local park courts.

Many people consider tennis one of the hardest sports to learn, due to the need for hand-eye coordination, flexibility, agility, strength, and speed. Players must master different shots and learn the mental side of the game, which is considered the hardest part. However, playing at any local court for practice is a fun and easy way to play the game.

Can I Become a Pro?

Becoming a pro might not seem as easy as you think.

Unlike other professions where you can plan to become a doctor or a lawyer you cannot really decide to become a tennis professional. The only thing you can decide is to embark in the process. Only a few top players have the privilege of earning a living competing, and the results throughout the developmental process will dictate who can join the club.
