What is tennis?

Tennis is a racquet sport that may be played with two teams of two players each or one player against one opponent (singles) (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racquet to strike a hollow rubber ball with felt covering over a net and into the court of their opponent.
Rackets
Rackets have many different variations. There are many brands to choose from, the best including Wilson and Babolat. Strings also matter, depending on the player. Polyester strings deliver more spin, while natural animal gut strings provide more power.
Courts
There are many different types of courts, but the most used are clay, grass, and hardcourt. Each has a different bounce pattern, with clay being the slowest, and grass being the fastest.
Tennis Balls
Tennis balls are covered with soft velvet, and have a rubber core. They usually compress a lot when hit, as they absorb the energy and rocket to the other side at maximum speeds of 163 MPH.
The Serve
The serve is amongst one of the 6 strokes in tennis. It is the fastest, and the most important in the game; An extremely fast serve can throw off your opponent.
